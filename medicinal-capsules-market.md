<p><p>The medicinal capsules market features two primary types: hard capsules and soft capsules. Hard capsules are typically composed of gelatin and are used to encase powdered or granular medication, allowing for easy swallowing and effective release in the digestive system. Soft capsules, on the other hand, have a gelatinous shell filled with liquid or semi-solid formulations, making them ideal for oils and potent medicines. Both types cater to patient preferences for dosage forms and play a crucial role in drug delivery.</p></p>
