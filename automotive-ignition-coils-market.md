<p><p>The automotive ignition coils market includes several types, each serving specific engine configurations. Can-type ignition coils are cylindrical and commonly used in older vehicles, providing reliable spark. Pencil ignition coils are slimmer and designed for space-constrained applications, often found in modern ignition systems. Double spark coils deliver a spark to two cylinders simultaneously, enhancing efficiency in certain engines. Additionally, other ignition coils encompass a range of specialized designs that cater to unique vehicle requirements, optimizing performance and emissions.</p></p>
