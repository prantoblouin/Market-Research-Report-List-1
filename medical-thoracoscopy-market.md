<p><p>The medical thoracoscopy market is categorized into two main types: rigid and semi-rigid instruments. Rigid thoracoscopy involves the use of a stiff, straight-endoscope that provides optimal visualization and access for procedures like biopsies and pleural fluid drainage. In contrast, semi-rigid thoracoscopy features a flexible design, allowing enhanced maneuverability within the thoracic cavity, which is beneficial for accessing difficult areas. Both types cater to different procedural needs, influencing their adoption based on clinical requirements and patient anatomy.</p></p>
